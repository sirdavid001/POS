Privacy policy

Effective June 11, 2026. This policy explains how QuickPOS collects, uses, and protects personal data under the Nigeria Data Protection Act 2023 and applicable guidance issued by the Nigeria Data Protection Commission.

Who we are and our role

The Data Controller responsible for QuickPOS account, subscription, billing, support, security, and website data is SIRDAVID MULTI-TRADE LTD (RC 9387137), the Nigerian company that operates QuickPOS.
Registered address: Asaba, Delta State, Nigeria.
Data protection contact: support@quickpos.name.ng.

For personal data that a store owner or authorised staff member enters into store records, the store determines why that data is used and is generally the Data Controller for that processing. QuickPOS processes that data on the store's instructions to provide the service and acts as a Data Processor or service provider where applicable. Store owners are responsible for having an appropriate lawful basis and providing any notice required to people whose personal data they enter into QuickPOS.

Lawful basis for processing

We process personal data only where a lawful basis under the Nigeria Data Protection Act 2023 applies, including:

  • Contract performance: processing account details, billing references, and store data is necessary to provide the QuickPOS service you have subscribed to.
  • Legitimate interests: we process operational logs, platform/version data, and support messages to diagnose failures, improve reliability, prevent fraud, and protect the security of the service.
  • Legal obligation: we retain certain records to meet applicable Nigerian financial, tax, and regulatory requirements.
  • Consent (where applicable): we obtain consent where required by law, for example before sending optional marketing communications, which we do not currently send.

Information we collect

We process the following categories of personal data:

  • Account and identity data: store name, owner name, email address, and hashed password.
  • Billing and payment data: subscription status, plan code, payment provider references, and transaction amounts. We do not store card numbers, CVVs, or full payment credentials — these are processed directly by Paystack or Flutterwave.
  • Store business data: products, orders, inventory records, staff details, settings, and reports you create in the app.
  • Legal acceptance records: the policy version accepted or acknowledged, acceptance time, IP address, user agent, and whether acceptance occurred during registration or checkout.
  • Technical data: app version, platform (OS), IP address (via hosting provider logs), and session tokens.
  • Support communications: messages and attachments you send to support.

Where information comes from

Most information is provided directly by store owners and authorised staff when they register, configure a store, use the application, complete checkout, or contact support. Payment status and provider references come from Paystack or Flutterwave. Technical and security information may be generated automatically by the app, browser, device, Vercel, Cloudflare, or our security logs when the service is accessed.

Store business data

QuickPOS processes store products, orders, inventory records, staff accounts, settings, and reports to operate the service. Store data is isolated by store account — no other store can access your data. Store owners control which staff members can access their store and at what permission level.

How information is used

Information is used to: authenticate users and manage sessions; deliver POS features and display your store data; verify and manage subscriptions; process payments through Paystack or Flutterwave; prevent fraud and abuse; send transactional emails (payment confirmations, password resets, store sales statements); respond to support requests; and diagnose failures and improve reliability.

Service providers and data sharing

We share your information only with service providers necessary to operate the service, and only to the extent required for the relevant purpose. Current providers include:

  • Vercel — hosting and edge network (website and API). Data may be processed on servers outside Nigeria.
  • Cloudflare — DNS and network security.
  • Resend — transactional email delivery (password resets, billing receipts, sales statements).
  • Paystack — payment processing. Paystack's own privacy policy and terms apply to data you submit on their checkout pages.
  • Flutterwave — payment processing. Flutterwave's own privacy policy and terms apply to data you submit on their checkout pages.
  • Vercel Analytics — aggregated, anonymised page-view analytics on the marketing website. No cookies are set for analytics purposes.

We do not sell your personal data to third parties. We do not share your data with third parties for their own marketing purposes.

International data transfers

Some service providers, including Vercel and Resend, operate international infrastructure and may process personal data outside Nigeria. We will make cross-border transfers only where permitted by the Nigeria Data Protection Act 2023, including where the destination provides adequate protection, appropriate safeguards apply, or another lawful transfer basis is available.

Cookies and analytics

The QuickPOS marketing website uses Vercel Web Analytics for basic website traffic measurement. Vercel Web Analytics is cookie-free, so QuickPOS does not currently set analytics cookies or display an analytics-cookie consent banner. The QuickPOS client application uses browser localStorage and session tokens for authentication; these are functional and necessary for the service to operate. If we introduce non-essential cookies or additional tracking, we will update this policy and request consent where required.

Children's data

QuickPOS is intended for use by business owners and their staff who are at least 18 years of age. We do not knowingly collect personal data from persons under 18. If you believe a minor has provided personal data through our service, please contact us so we can take appropriate action.

Data retention

Account and store records are retained while an account is active and afterward only for as long as reasonably necessary to complete account closure, support data export, resolve disputes, enforce agreements, prevent fraud, and meet legal or regulatory obligations. Billing transactions, payment references, legal acceptance records, and security logs may be retained for a longer period where needed for financial records, legal claims, audit, or compliance. Password-reset tokens expire and are not retained as reusable credentials. When information is no longer required, it is deleted or anonymised using the processes available to the relevant system.

Security and personal data incidents

We use access controls, store-level data isolation, signed and expiring session tokens, password hashing, provider webhook signature verification, and encrypted network transport (TLS) to protect personal data. Access is limited according to assigned staff roles. No system can guarantee absolute security, so users must also protect account credentials and devices. If a personal data breach creates a notification obligation under applicable law, we will notify the Nigeria Data Protection Commission and affected data subjects as required.

Automated decision-making

QuickPOS does not currently make decisions that produce legal or similarly significant effects about individuals solely through automated processing. Automated subscription checks may place a store in read-only mode when a verified trial or paid period expires, but the owner can review billing status, contact support, and restore access through a verified payment.

Your rights as a data subject

Under the Nigeria Data Protection Act 2023, you may exercise the following rights, subject to applicable legal exceptions:

  • Access: the right to request a copy of personal data we hold about you.
  • Correction: the right to request that inaccurate or incomplete data be corrected.
  • Erasure: the right to request deletion of your personal data where it is no longer necessary for the purposes for which it was collected, subject to legal and operational retention requirements.
  • Restriction: the right to request that we restrict processing in certain circumstances.
  • Objection: the right to object to processing based on legitimate interests.
  • Portability: the right to receive your data in a structured, commonly used, machine-readable format where technically feasible.
  • Withdraw consent: where processing is based on consent, the right to withdraw consent at any time without affecting the lawfulness of prior processing.

Store owners may update account and store information directly in the app. To exercise any right listed above, contact support@quickpos.name.ng. We may request information needed to verify your identity and will respond within the period required by applicable law. You also have the right to lodge a complaint with the Nigeria Data Protection Commission (NDPC) if you believe your data protection rights have been violated.
